# Persona: Dana Choi

## Demographics
- **Age:** 38
- **Title:** VP of Operations / Head of Ops at a Series B SaaS startup (80–200 employees)
- **Reports to:** CEO or COO
- **Location:** Austin, TX (remote-first company, team spread across 3 time zones)
- **Income:** $190K base + equity
- **Education:** MBA from a mid-tier program; undergrad in business or industrial engineering

## Psychographics
- Operator mindset — obsessed with systems, processes, and removing friction
- Reads Lenny's Newsletter, follows operators on Twitter/X, lurks in private Slack communities for ops leaders
- Thinks in spreadsheets but wishes she could think in dashboards
- Skeptical of hype but genuinely excited about tools that save her team hours
- Quietly ambitious — wants to be the person who "figured it out" before the board had to ask

## What She Likes
- Tools that work out of the box with minimal config
- Clear, honest pricing — no "contact sales" walls
- Case studies with real numbers (not vanity metrics)
- Products that respect her intelligence and don't talk down to her
- Founders who have actually operated a company, not just raised money
- Integration with tools she already uses (Slack, Notion, Rippling, Greenhouse, Google Workspace)
- Being able to trial something solo before dragging her team into a demo

## What She Dislikes
- Buzzword-heavy marketing ("AI-native," "paradigm shift," "revolutionary")
- Products that require a 6-week implementation and a dedicated CSM
- Vendors that feel like they're selling to her CEO instead of to her
- Anything that creates MORE work for her team before it creates less
- Dark-pattern pricing / hidden costs that surface after onboarding
- Being made to feel like her job is at risk by the tool she's evaluating
- Edgy/snarky brand voice that treats real problems like a joke

## Problems She's Trying to Solve
1. **Hiring is a bottleneck.** Open roles sit for 45+ days. Hiring managers complain to her. Recruiters are overwhelmed. She's duct-taping the process with spreadsheets and Slack reminders.
2. **Performance reviews are a fire drill.** Every quarter it's a scramble. Managers copy-paste last cycle's feedback. The data lives in 4 different places. She knows the process is broken but doesn't have time to fix it.
3. **Comp decisions are gut-feel.** She has no reliable benchmarking. Every raise/offer negotiation is a coin flip. She's terrified of losing good people because she can't show them a credible number.
4. **Compliance gives her anxiety.** The company is growing into new states/countries. She's not confident their handbook or policies are up to date. She can't afford a full-time employment lawyer yet.
5. **She's drowning in manual work.** Onboarding checklists, offboarding workflows, benefits enrollment reminders — all held together with Notion docs and hope. She needs to automate but every "automation tool" she's tried added complexity instead of removing it.

## Her Internal Monologue When Evaluating Tools
> "Just show me what it does. Don't make me sit through a 30-minute demo to find out it doesn't integrate with Greenhouse. Give me a free trial, a clear pricing page, and one real customer who looks like me. If it works, I'll sell it to my CEO myself — I don't need your sales team to do that."

## What Would Make Her Sign Up Today
- A free trial or sandbox she can poke around in for 15 minutes
- One compelling use case that matches her #1 pain (probably hiring or performance reviews)
- A pricing page that doesn't require a call
- A testimonial from someone at a company her size, with her title, solving her problem
- Confidence that this won't be another tool her team resents her for buying
